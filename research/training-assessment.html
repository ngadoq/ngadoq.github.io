Human-Centered Design for Game-Based Assessments

Applied human-centered design principles to improve candidate experience and assessment validity for a game-based hiring tools - then evaluated the intervention's effectiveness through rigorous experimental methods.

Organization HireVue
Role External Researcher
Timeline Oct 2021 - Dec 2023

Overview

Game-based assessments are an innovative approach to talent selection - using interactive tasks to measure cognitive abilities, personality, and problem-solving in ways that traditional interviews and tests cannot. But innovation in methodology means nothing if candidates don't understand what they're doing or why.

I was engaged as part of a research team to design a training module that would bridge this gap - improving candidate understanding and experience while preserving the psychometric validity of the assessments themselves.

Approach

  • Applied human-centered design principles throughout - starting with an understanding of the candidate experience, identifying pain points in how candidates encountered and interpreted the game-based tasks.
  • Designed a training module to orient candidates to the assessment format, reduce anxiety, and set accurate expectations without coaching to the construct being measured.
  • Balanced transparency with validity: the module needed to improve experience without inflating or deflating scores by tipping off candidates to what was being assessed.
  • Evaluated the training module's effectiveness using rigorous experimental design - comparing candidate experience, performance distributions, and assessment validity metrics across conditions.
  • Synthesized findings into evidence-based product recommendations delivered to stakeholders.

Impact

This project sits at the intersection of psychometric science, human-centered design, and product development - an application of I-O psychology expertise directly to hiring technology. The recommendations informed product iterations to the game-based assessment candidate-facing experience.

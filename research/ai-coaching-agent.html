Research Project · In Progress · University of Minnesota

AI Coaching Agent for Leadership Development

Designed an LLM-powered coaching agent that delivers personalized leadership interventions - grounding AI capability in psychological theory of goal-setting and coaching.

Timeline 2025 - Present
Role Lead Researcher & Designer
Status In Progress

The Problem

Leadership development at scale is hard. Traditional coaching is expensive, inconsistent, and hard to evaluate. This project asks: can an LLM-powered agent deliver psychologically grounded, personalized coaching that actually works?

I designed and evaluated a coaching agent that adapts its interventions to individual leaders based on their goals, context, and growth trajectories — combining behavioral science principles with modern LLM capabilities.

Approach

  • Grounded the agent's intervention logic in established leadership development theory (goal-setting, self-determination theory, reflective questioning).
  • Built the agent using OpenAI API, with a memory system to track progress across sessions.
  • Ran qualitative user interviews with 20+ participants to evaluate perceived usefulness, trust, and usability.
  • Designed a controlled experiment to measure longitudinal behavioral outcomes change.

Impact

The agent is currently being scaled for deployment at the U.S. Naval Academy, where it will support leadership development for incoming officers. This transition from prototype to real-world deployment required significant work on reliability, safety guardrails, and evaluation frameworks.
